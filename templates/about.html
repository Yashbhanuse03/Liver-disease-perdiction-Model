About the Liver Disease Prediction System

Overview

The Liver Disease Prediction System is a machine learning-based application designed to predict the likelihood of liver disease based on various medical parameters. This system uses a trained model to analyze patient data and provide a prediction of whether the patient may have liver disease.

How It Works

The system uses a machine learning model trained on the Indian Liver Patient Dataset (ILPD). The model analyzes the following parameters:

  • Age and Gender
  • Bilirubin levels (Total and Direct)
  • Enzyme levels (Alkaline Phosphotase, Alamine Aminotransferase, Aspartate Aminotransferase)
  • Protein levels (Total Proteins, Albumin)
  • Albumin and Globulin Ratio

Based on these parameters, the model calculates the probability of liver disease and provides a prediction.

Model Performance

The prediction model has been trained and evaluated on a dataset of liver patients. The model achieves:

  • Accuracy: ~69%
  • Precision: ~73% for detecting no disease
  • Recall: ~90% for detecting no disease

About Liver Disease

Liver disease refers to any condition that damages the liver and affects its function. The liver is responsible for many critical functions within the body, including:

  • Filtering the blood
  • Processing nutrients, medications, and hormones
  • Producing bile to help digest fats
  • Storing vitamins and minerals
  • Removing toxins from the body

Common types of liver disease include hepatitis, fatty liver disease, cirrhosis, and liver cancer. Early detection and treatment of liver disease can help prevent progression to more serious conditions.

Risk Factors for Liver Disease

Several factors can increase the risk of developing liver disease:

  • Heavy alcohol consumption
  • Obesity
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Viral infections (hepatitis A, B, C)
  • Exposure to certain chemicals or toxins
  • Family history of liver disease
